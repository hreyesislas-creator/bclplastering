# Post-launch playbook

The site is live. Now the work is to feed it: reviews, projects,
local citations, and fast lead response. This document is the
30-60-90 day operating manual.

## The single most important habit

> **Respond to every lead within 30 minutes during business hours.**

Rapid response is the highest-leverage thing this business can do.
Industry data: leads are 21× more likely to convert if contacted
within 5 minutes vs. 30 minutes. Most contractors respond in hours
or days. Beating that is a free moat.

How:
- New-lead emails arrive instantly via Resend.
- Open the dashboard `/dashboard/leads/<id>` from the email link.
- Tap **Call** or **WhatsApp** from the detail page within 30 min.
- Move status from `new` → `contacted` so the pipeline reflects it.

## Review collection cadence

The website's `/reviews` page already invites homeowners to review
on Google, Yelp, and Facebook. Convert that prompt into a system:

1. **Day of final walk-through** — text the homeowner the direct
   Google review link. One sentence: "Quick favor — would you
   leave us a 5★ if everything met your expectations? Two
   minutes here: <link>."
2. **Day +3** — if no review yet, follow up once with a different
   channel (email if the SMS didn't work, vice versa).
3. **Day +30** — share the project on Instagram + GBP "Updates"
   tagging the customer (with permission). Keeps the relationship
   warm for repeat work and referrals.

Goal cadence: **2 new Google reviews / week**. At 8 / month, the
business will hit 250+ reviews within a year — a moat that's
genuinely hard for competitors to match.

## Content rhythm

Two posts per week, alternating:

| Day | Channel | Asset | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | Instagram + GBP | Before/after carousel | New project from `/dashboard/projects` |
| Friday | GBP "Updates" | Customer quote + 1 photo | New review from `/dashboard/reviews` |

Post-add to the dashboard *first*, then share. That way the
website is the source of truth and the structured data updates
automatically. Re-using the same image and copy across channels is
fine and actually helps SEO.

### Project capture protocol

Train the crew lead to take photos on every job:
- 4–6 wide shots of the existing condition (BEFORE) before they
  break ground.
- 4–6 matching wide shots (AFTER) at final walk-through.
- 2 detail shots showing the trade craft (texture closeups).

Same focal length, same time of day if possible. Crisp before/after
pairs are 80% of the marketing value of every project.

## Local SEO — first 90 days

### Week 1
- [ ] Google Business Profile published + verified (postcard).
- [ ] Bing Places mirrored.
- [ ] Apple Maps Connect listing.
- [ ] Yelp claim + populate.
- [ ] Thumbtack and Houzz business pages.
- [ ] **Five core citations** (NAP must match exactly):
      Yellow Pages, BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Foursquare.

### Weeks 2–4
- [ ] Set up GBP weekly post cadence (above).
- [ ] First three Google reviews from past customers via direct text.
- [ ] **Search Console:** verify, submit sitemap, watch coverage.
- [ ] **Geo-targeted service pages** if traffic stalls — landing
      pages like `/stucco-repair-riverside` only when there's
      enough unique content to justify them. Don't spawn empty
      pages for every city × service combo.

### Months 2–3
- [ ] 20+ Google reviews (2 / week pace).
- [ ] 10 published projects on the website.
- [ ] One local press mention or HOA Facebook-group share.
- [ ] **Backlink push:** local newspaper interview, BNI / Chamber
      of Commerce listing, customer-blog mentions.

## Service-area expansion

Resist expanding to LA / OC until the Inland Empire pipeline is at
capacity. Geographic focus = better Google rankings + tighter crew
logistics + faster job turnover.

When ready to expand:
1. Add the new city to **Dashboard → Settings → Service areas**.
   The footer + local-business JSON-LD update automatically.
2. Publish 1–2 projects in the new city before claiming it on GBP.
3. Build a local citation set (steps from "Week 1" above) targeting
   the new city.
4. Track each city's lead share monthly. Cities below 5% of leads
   for two consecutive quarters → drop them and tighten the focus.

## Growth metrics to track

Weekly review (Sunday evening, 15 minutes):

| Metric | Source | Healthy range |
|---|---|---|
| New leads / week | `/dashboard` overview | 5+ |
| Lead → estimate conversion | leads pipeline | ≥ 70% |
| Estimate → won conversion | leads pipeline | ≥ 25% |
| New Google reviews / week | GBP | 1+ |
| Avg. response time | Manual log | < 30 min |
| Site sessions / week | GA4 | growing month over month |
| Lead form submission rate | GA4 conversions | ≥ 2% of sessions |

When any metric drops two weeks in a row, look at the channel:
- Drop in leads but stable sessions → conversion issue (CTAs / form).
- Drop in sessions → SEO issue (rankings / GBP / reviews).
- Drop in conversion rate → response speed or estimate quality.

## Yearly milestones

| Mark | Target |
|---|---|
| Month 6 | 100 Google reviews, 25 published projects |
| Month 12 | 250 Google reviews, 60 published projects, ranking top-3 in maps for "stucco contractor Riverside" |

The platform you have today is the lead engine. Filling it with
real reviews and real before/afters every week is what turns it
into a dominant local brand within twelve months.
